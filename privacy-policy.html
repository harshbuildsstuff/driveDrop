Privacy Policy
Last updated — June 2025
This page explains what DriveDrop does with your data when you use the app. The short version — we don't collect, store, or share anything about you. Read on for the full picture.
01
What DriveDrop Is
DriveDrop is a personal web app hosted on GitHub Pages. It lets you upload files from your device directly to your own Google Drive account. No backend server is involved — the app runs entirely in your browser and communicates with Google's servers on your behalf.
02
What Data We Access
When you sign in with Google, DriveDrop requests access to your Google Drive. This access is used for three things only — uploading files you choose, reading your storage quota to display how much space you have left, and listing your Drive folders so you can pick a destination. Nothing else is read, copied, or inspected.
Your files never touch our servers. because there are no servers😄. Uploads go from your device straight to Google Drive , using your own access token.
03
What We Store Locally
DriveDrop stores a small amount of data in your browser's localStorage. This includes your cookie consent preference, your upload statistics (total files uploaded, total data sent), and any achievements you have unlocked. This data never leaves your device and is not sent anywhere.
04
What We Do Not Do
We do not collect your name, email address, or any personal details. We do not log your uploads or track which files you send. We do not sell, share, or transfer any information to third parties. We do not use analytics tools or advertising trackers of any kind.
05
Google OAuth and Permissions
Signing in uses Google's OAuth 2.0 system. The access token Google provides is stored in memory only for the duration of your session — it is gone the moment you close or refresh the page. DriveDrop uses the drive scope, which covers file uploads, storage quota, and folder listing. You can revoke this access at any time by visiting your Google Account permissions page.
06
Third Party Services
DriveDrop uses the following external services — Google Identity Services for authentication, the Google Drive API for file operations, and Google Fonts for typography. Each of these is governed by Google's own privacy policies. DriveDrop has no control over how Google processes data on its end.
07
Children's Privacy
DriveDrop is not directed at children under the age of 13. The app does not knowingly collect any data from minors. If you believe a minor has used this app and you have concerns, please reach out using the contact details below.
08
Changes to This Policy
If this privacy policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date at the top. Continuing to use DriveDrop after a change is posted means you accept the updated terms.
